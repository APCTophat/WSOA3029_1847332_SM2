“all I hear is the wind slapping against the gravestones of those who did not make it,”

This first line really opened my eyes to how I perceive, tragedy and suffering. I remember repeatedly saying to friends and family things like, “Don’t worry we will make it through this pandemic” or “We only have to not see each other for a couple months, then we can throw a big party”. And the truth is, if everyone said this, some would be lying.

Currently the total global death toll is . Sure, comparing that to people that have recovered it ‘doesn’t seem so bad’ but that’s bullshit. That is people whose lives were stolen, just because ‘they’ are people who are far away or people you can’t even perceive. Does not mean it isn’t tragic or can be treated as a simple statistic. The world lost mothers, fathers, sons, daughter, lovers, best friends and loved ones. The wind is slapping new gravestones, never forget that.
