"Some of us have become ghosts by the time the dust has settled."

I chose this quote because I feel there is a notion of ‘the dust settling’ with Covid-19. With the holiday season coming up I feel people are relaxing more and becoming careless. However, I believe that the dust will only settle once most of the global population is immune and we are far from that happening right now.

I wanted to focus on the ‘Ghosts’ that Covid-19 has created and how, like ghosts, they fade into the background of our minds sometimes to the point we forget. Taking inspiration from the quote I wanted to showcase the idea of the ‘deaths’ graph haunting the ‘recovered’ graph.

the quote I wanted to showcase the idea of the ‘deaths’ graph haunting the ‘recovered’ graph. To do this, the graph consists of two overlayed bar graphs, showcasing the number of ‘recovered’ and ‘deaths’ in South Africa from Covid-19 over several months. The graph contains three view modes which can be accessed through a panel below it.

The first view mode ‘Recovered’ is the default view for this graph. The recovered portion is shown on a scale of 0 to 300 000 with colour to indicate the month and an index that shows the exact number documented for the day. Behind this graph is the ghost graph on a 0 to 7000 scale, which is slightly opaque in the bars and text values to emphasise the ‘ghostly’ nature of being there whilst also not being there. I found that the highest value for recovered and 300000 were in a similar ratio with the highest death value and 7000.

The second view mode brings the ‘ghosts’ graph to the forefront by removing the ‘recovered’ visuals and solidifying the ‘deaths’ visuals. I also changed all the colours to be grey to make the barest, non-sugar-coated representation. In this mode, the graph and value texts should be more legible and force the viewer to acknowledge it.

The final mode ‘comparison’ is my tongue in cheek graph representing typical comparison graphs that can be found on the internet or news media. Where the things being compared are vastly different that one element becomes overshadowed and lost. For this graph, both graphs are ranged from 0 to 300000. This obviously means nothing of recovered has changed but the ‘ghost’ graph has practically disappeared. To emphasise this disappearance more, I completely removed the value of the deaths from the index. I wanted this graph to comment on the tendency to only focus on the recoveries and thinking that everything is okay. Making all that died truly become ghosts, not acknowledging its existence.

Right now, nearing the end of November 2020, ‘they’ say there is a light at the end of the tunnel, but we are leaving the ghosts of loved ones behind in the tunnel, never to escape. We leave those who did not make it to see the dust settle. If we forget them then they will be truly lost.
